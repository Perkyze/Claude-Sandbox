# Change Request Policy (Scope Management)

**Goal**: Maintain clear boundaries, protect your margins, and keep clients happy without overdelivering.

---

## What's Included in Base Package

### Starter Package ($2,500)
- **4 pages** as defined in approved sitemap
- **2 rounds of design revisions** (minor layout, color, content tweaks)
- **Standard features**: Contact form, Google Maps, mobile responsive, basic SEO
- **30 days post-launch support**: Bug fixes, minor content updates (<1 hr total)

### Pro Package ($4,000)
- Everything in Starter, PLUS:
- **6 pages** (2 additional pages)
- **Booking system integration** (Amelia, Calendly, or Cal.com)
- **Blog setup** (template + 1 demo post)
- **Advanced SEO**: Schema markup, Google Business Profile setup
- **1-hour training video** (how to manage site)

---

## What's NOT Included (Out of Scope)

These require **additional quotes** (hourly rate: $100/hr or fixed-price add-on):

### Major Design Changes
- Complete redesign of approved layout
- Changing color scheme after 2nd revision round
- Adding new sections not in original sitemap (e.g., "Can we add a portfolio gallery?")

### New Pages or Features
- Pages beyond agreed count (4 for Starter, 6 for Pro)
- E-commerce functionality (products, cart, checkout)
- Custom integrations (CRM, payment gateway, third-party API)
- Membership or login system

### Content Creation
- **Writing copy**: $150 per page (if client doesn't provide text)
- **Photography**: $400 for half-day shoot (or use stock photos)
- **Logo design**: $600 (if client doesn't have one)
- **Video editing**: Quote per project

### Complex Functionality
- Multi-language site (beyond basic plugin setup)
- Custom animations or interactive elements
- Advanced forms (multi-step, conditional logic, payment)
- Database-driven features (custom post types, filters)

### Ongoing Maintenance (After 30 Days)
- Content updates (text, images, menu changes)
- WordPress/plugin updates
- Security monitoring
- Backups and uptime checks
- **→ Offer Care Plan ($200/month) instead**

## How to Handle Change Requests

### Step 1: Client Makes Request
**Channels**: Email, Slack, project management tool (Trello, Asana)

**Example**:
> "Hey, can we add a testimonials slider to the homepage?"

### Step 2: Assess Scope
**Ask yourself**:
- Is this in the original sitemap? (No)
- Does it fit within "minor revisions"? (No, it's a new feature)
- Time estimate: <30 min or >30 min? (>30 min to build + test)

**Decision**:
- **<30 min + related to approved scope** → Include for free (goodwill gesture)
- **>30 min OR new feature** → Quote as add-on

### Step 3: Communicate Estimate
**Email Template** (Professional + Friendly):

**Subject**: `Re: Testimonials Slider Request`

**Body**:
> Bonjour {{name}},
>
> Bonne idée! Un slider de témoignages va vraiment ajouter de la crédibilité à votre page d'accueil.
>
> **Estimation**:
> - Temps: 1-2 heures (design + code + responsive test)
> - Coût: $150 (forfait) OU $100/heure (facturation réelle)
>
> **Inclus**:
> - Slider avec 3-5 témoignages
> - Auto-rotate ou navigation manuelle
> - Responsive (mobile + desktop)
>
> Si vous voulez procéder, répondez "Approuvé" et je l'ajoute à votre timeline (livraison: +1 jour).
>
> Merci,
> {{your_name}}

### Step 4: Get Written Approval
**Never start work without approval**—even if client says "yeah, sounds good" verbally.

**Require**:
- Email reply with "Approved" or "Go ahead"
- Updated budget confirmation

**Why**: Protects you from "I didn't think it would cost that much" disputes.

### Step 5: Track Change Orders
**Document in project file** (`outputs/sites/[client]/change-orders.md`):

```markdown
## Change Order #1 (2025-11-18)
**Request**: Add testimonials slider to homepage
**Estimated**: 2 hours @ $100/hr = $200
**Approved**: Yes (email 2025-11-18)
**Status**: Completed
**Actual Time**: 1.5 hours
**Invoice**: $200 (billed with final 50% payment)

## Change Order #2 (2025-11-20)
**Request**: Add "Team" page (not in original brief)
**Estimated**: 3 hours @ $100/hr = $300
**Approved**: No (client declined, removed from scope)
**Status**: Canceled
```

## Handling Difficult Situations

### Scenario #1: "This should be included!"
**Client says**: "But I assumed a contact form would have file upload! Why is that extra?"

**Your response**:
> "Je comprends la confusion. Le package de base inclut un formulaire de contact standard (nom, email, message). L'ajout d'upload de fichiers nécessite un plugin supplémentaire et des tests de sécurité—c'est pourquoi c'est un add-on ($100).
>
> Si c'est essentiel pour vous, je peux l'ajouter. Sinon, on peut lancer avec le formulaire standard et l'ajouter plus tard si besoin."

**Key**: Reference original brief/proposal, stay calm, offer options.

### Scenario #2: "Can you just do it for free this time?"
**Client says**: "It's just a small thing, can you include it as a favor?"

**Your response** (if < 30 min):
> "Bien sûr, je peux le faire cette fois. C'est rapide et ça va améliorer le site. Mais pour les prochaines demandes comme ça, on devra facturer—juste pour être transparent!"

**Your response** (if > 30 min):
> "J'aimerais bien, mais ça représente 2 heures de travail et ça affecte mes autres projets. Je peux vous faire un prix réduit ($150 au lieu de $200) si vous approuvez aujourd'hui?"

**Key**: Set precedent early—free favors become expectations.

### Scenario #3: "I didn't know it would be this expensive!"
**Client says**: "You quoted $300 for this feature but it's not worth it."

**Your response**:
> "Je comprends. L'estimation est basée sur le temps réel pour développer, tester, et intégrer. Si le budget est serré, on peut:
> 1. Reporter cette fonctionnalité à la Phase 2 (après lancement)
> 2. Simplifier le design (version plus basique pour $150)
> 3. Ne pas l'inclure du tout
>
> Quelle option préférez-vous?"

**Key**: Offer alternatives, don't drop price without reducing scope.

## Preventing Scope Creep

### Prevention Strategy #1: Detailed Briefs
- Use comprehensive questionnaire (`prompts/brief-questionnaire.md`)
- Get client approval on **written sitemap** before coding
- Clarify what's included vs. excluded upfront

### Prevention Strategy #2: Educate Early
**In proposal**, include section:
> **What's NOT included** (can be added for additional fees):
> - Extra pages beyond [count]
> - E-commerce functionality
> - Content writing
> - Custom integrations
> - Ongoing maintenance after 30 days

### Prevention Strategy #3: Weekly Check-Ins
- Send progress updates (screenshots, staging links)
- Ask: "Any feedback so far?" (catch issues early)
- Remind: "We're in Week 1 of 2—any changes now are easier than later"

### Prevention Strategy #4: Clear Revision Policy
**In contract**:
> **Revisions**: 2 rounds included (minor layout, color, content adjustments). Major redesigns or new features are billed separately at $100/hour.

---

## When to Say No

**Red flags** (decline or charge premium):
- Client asks for 5th round of revisions (beyond included 2)
- Request fundamentally changes agreed design ("Can we switch from modern to vintage theme?")
- Scope doubles mid-project ("Actually, we need 10 pages instead of 4")
- Client delays project, then demands rush delivery

**How to decline professionally**:
> "Je comprends que vos besoins ont évolué, mais ça dépasse significativement le scope original. Je peux vous faire une nouvelle proposition pour ce projet élargi, ou on peut terminer le projet actuel et planifier une Phase 2. Qu'est-ce qui fonctionne mieux?"

## Summary: Stay Firm, Stay Professional

**Core Principles**:
1. **Document everything**: Briefs, approvals, change orders
2. **Communicate early**: Estimate before starting work
3. **Offer options**: Premium feature vs. simplified version vs. defer to Phase 2
4. **Protect your time**: Your hourly rate reflects expertise, not just task time
5. **Build goodwill**: Throw in small freebies (<30 min) occasionally, but set limits

**Goal**: Deliver excellent work, maintain profitability, and train clients to respect your process.
